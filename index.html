Welcome

I am a social scientist specialized in the European Convention on Human Rights, international litigation, and human rights protection in Russia. I defended my PhD in sociology at the University of Helsinki, Finland in 2014. I design human rights research with an eye to human interest and enjoy carrying out in-depth interviews. My work is driven by the question how human rights lawyers use international courts, for instance the European Court of Human Rights, to help victims of grave atrocities to find effective remedies, to prevent future violations, or incite domestic legal reform.

I have over seven years of experience in conducting local fieldwork and human rights research in Russia. During those years I have carried out interviews with young politicians, youth activists, political party members, victims, human rights lawyers, and experts working for NGOs. At the moment I am working on a study on the protection of human rights lawyers and journalists.

Publications

My publications cover the protection of human rights defenders in Russia and the Russian cases before the European Court of Human Rights on the second conflict in Chechnya, on torture, and on discrimination of Roma.

Peer-reviewed articles

Van der Vet, Freek and Lyytikäinen, Laura. 2015. "Violence and Human Rights in Russia: How Human Rights Defenders Develop their Tactics in the Face of Danger, 2005-2013". The International Journal of Human Rights 19 (7): 979-998

This article appeared in the (now Free Access) Special Issue "Critical Perspectives on the Security and Protection of Human Rights Defenders" of the International Journal of Human Rights.

The United Nations Special Rapporteur on the Situation of Human Rights Defenders endorsed the issue:

"In the face of growing attacks on human rights in many countries, the risks faced by people who defend their universality have intensified. Human rights defenders increasingly face criminalization, persecution, arbitrary arrest and detention. They are threatened, physically attacked and assassinated. As we mark the 17th anniversary of the UN Declaration on Human Rights Defenders we should ask ourselves what more must be done to better protect defenders and promote their work. I welcome this Special Issue of the International Journal of Human Rights. It compels us to confront these challenges and to discuss critical questions, both among scholars and practitioners. Our next common goal is to translate this research into practical tools to strengthen the protection regime for human rights defenders", Michel Forst, UN Special Rapporteur on the Situation of Human Rights Defenders
